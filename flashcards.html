Brain Flashcards

What is the function of the frontal lobe?
The frontal lobe controls decision-making, planning, problem-solving, and voluntary motor control. It is also associated with personality and speech production.
What does the parietal lobe process?
The parietal lobe processes sensory information such as touch, temperature, and pain. It helps with spatial awareness and coordination.
What is the role of the occipital lobe?
The occipital lobe is primarily responsible for visual processing, interpreting information like color, shape, and motion.
What is the function of the hypothalamus?
The hypothalamus regulates critical functions such as hunger, thirst, sleep, and emotional activity. It also controls the autonomic nervous system.
What does the amygdala do?
The amygdala is involved in emotional responses, memory processing, and decision-making. It plays a key role in fear and pleasure responses.
What is the function of the hippocampus?
The hippocampus is crucial for memory formation and spatial navigation. It helps convert short-term memories into long-term ones.
What does the temporal lobe process?
The temporal lobe processes auditory information, memory, and language comprehension. It contains the hippocampus and Wernicke’s area.
What is the primary role of the cerebellum?
The cerebellum is responsible for coordination, balance, and fine motor control. It also plays a role in cognitive functions and motor learning.
What is the function of the brainstem?
The brainstem controls vital functions like heart rate, breathing, and blood pressure. It also connects the brain to the spinal cord.
What role does the thalamus play in the brain?
The thalamus acts as a relay center, transmitting sensory and motor signals to the cerebral cortex and regulating sleep and alertness.
What does the limbic system regulate?
The limbic system regulates emotions, memory, and motivation. It includes structures like the hippocampus, amygdala, and cingulate gyrus.
What is the function of Broca’s area?
Broca’s area, located in the frontal lobe, is responsible for speech production and articulation.
What does Wernicke’s area control?
Wernicke’s area, located in the temporal lobe, is essential for language comprehension and processing.
What is the function of cerebrospinal fluid?
Cerebrospinal fluid cushions the brain and spinal cord, circulates nutrients, and removes waste products.
What is the corpus callosum?
The corpus callosum is a thick bundle of nerve fibers that connects the left and right hemispheres of the brain, facilitating communication between them.
What is the role of the medulla oblongata?
The medulla oblongata controls involuntary functions like breathing, heart rate, and digestion. It is part of the brainstem.
